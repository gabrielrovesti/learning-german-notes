# **1. The Three Genders (Genus)**

Every noun in German has one of the three genders:

- **der** → masculine
    
- **die** → feminine
    
- **das** → neuter
    

**Examples (all from your materials):**

- _der_ **Tag**
    
- _die_ **Ente**
    
- _das_ **Haus** (appears in Unit 2951)
    

Gender must be memorized with the noun.

---

# **2. The Two Article Types**

## **2.1 Definite Articles (“the”)**

| Case       | Masc    | Fem     | Neut    | Plural  |
| ---------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| Nominative | **der** | **die** | **das** | **die** |
| Accusative | **den** | **die** | **das** | **die** |
| Dative     | **dem** | **der** | **dem** | **den** |
| Genitive   | **des** | **der** | **des** | **der** |

You have already seen **dem / den** in your alphabet exercises:

- “dem – den – the” (Übung 1)
    

---

## **2.2 Indefinite Articles (“a / an”)**

| Case       | Masc      | Fem       | Neut      | Plural |
| ---------- | --------- | --------- | --------- | ------ |
| Nominative | **ein**   | **eine**  | **ein**   | —      |
| Accusative | **einen** | **eine**  | **ein**   | —      |
| Dative     | **einem** | **einer** | **einem** | —      |
| Genitive   | **eines** | **einer** | **eines** | —      |

No plural form for _ein_, like in English.

# **3. When to Use Each Case**

German articles change depending on the sentence role.

## **3.1 Nominative (subject)**

Who does the action?

- **Der Mann** geht.
    
- **Die Frau** arbeitet.
    
- **Das Haus** ist groß.
    

Appears in greetings dialogues when identifying subjects:  
“**Andrea und Bernd sind Freunde**.”

---

## **3.2 Accusative (direct object)**

Who / what receives the action?

- Ich sehe **den Mann**.
    
- Ich treffe **eine Freundin**.
    
- Wir kaufen **das Buch**.
    

You saw it implicitly in:  
„Ich wünsche Ihnen **einen schönen Abend**.“ (Herr Daum → Frau Schulz, Unit 2951)

---

## **3.3 Dative (indirect object / after specific prepositions)**

Typical prepositions: **mit, bei, nach, aus, zu, von**.

Examples from your materials:

- „**Kommen Sie gut nach Hause!**“ (nach + Dativ → _nach Hause_ fixed form)
    
- „Ich habe noch viel **zu tun**.“ (zu + infinitive, fixed construction)
    

Dative shows up VERY early in German.

---

## **3.4 Genitive (possession)**

Rare in speech.  
Replaced often by **von + dative**.

- Das Auto **des Mannes**.
    
- Die Tasche **der Frau**.
    

Not yet in your beginner documents, but necessary for mastery.

---

# **4. Plurals + Articles**

Plurals always use **die** in nominative and accusative:

- **die Freunde**  
    In your materials:  
    „Sind Andrea und Bernd **Freunde**?“
    

Plural dative ends in **-n** (usually):

- mit **den Freunden**
    

---

# **5. Practical Patterns to Memorize**

## **5.1 Masculine is the only one that changes in accusative**

- der → **den**
    
- ein → **einen**
    

Everything else stays the same.

## **5.2 Plural dative ALWAYS attracts an -n**

- den Kindern
    
- den Freunden
    

## **5.3 Feminine is easiest: it is ALWAYS “die / eine” in nominative and accusative**
